          We went here for lunch on a weekend. Unfortunately when we reached they had no tables however they did offer to seat us at the bar. Since we'd been looking forward to visiting this place for a while we agreed and luckily a table did open up in the next 5minutes. I loved the look of the restaurant- the walls and cutlery were colourful and it was just a nice place to sit and have lunch at.

        We ordered the patty Bao and dancing prawns to begin with. Since they had a seafood special week on at the moment we also ordered a soft shell crab dish. I loved all three dishes. The crab was exceptionally good. For the main course we tried the Malaysian chicken Kapitan and the curried flat rice noodles with seafood and I loved these two dishes too.

        To end, unfortunately certain desserts weren't available. We ended up ordering the warm fig and candied ginger pudding and the banana cake with jasmine rice pudding. While I quite liked the former, I thought the latter was quite so so.

        Service was good too. Though I ordered the peach fizzler to drink and quite liked it (unlike mocktails in other restaurants it wasn't loaded with ice) my mum liked the look of a cocktail being served at the adjacent table and since she doesn't have alcohol, they offered to make it for her as a mocktail instead.

        This is a place I'd definitely visit again.

          Finally this restaurant is ticked off from my list. Okay so lets start with the ambience. Classy is the ultimate word to describe the ambience. The soothing music and dim lights will fill your heart with romanticism. A perfect place for date I must say. And if you able to manage a seat at rooftop then no one can stop you from visiting this place again and again.
        Now the food. Mmmmmm I am puzzled now don't know where to start. We concentrated on drink and appetizers on that night so that we can try the most of their exclusive dishes ;).The cocktails- Bora Bora and Mandalay Bay. The addition of coconut water makes Bora Bora a special one. Presence of jasmine tea in Mandalay Bay was just perfect. The fried squid was very tasty. After eating the shaking beef we were also shaking with joy. I never thought that beef could be so tender. Now coming to the Sushis. My God they serves the best Sushis in town. The California roll was so nice. Made of crab meat it will just melt inside your mouth. I had Nigiri Sushi for the first time. It was just awesome. We opted salmon balsamic glaze sushi in their Nigiri Sushi section. The Fatty Bao PB&J was the real star for that night. A well balanced portion of pork belly served with traditional miso jam. The taste of that miso jam is fabulous. This must be present in your list when you are visiting this place. Well their speciality is in serving different types of Baos. So we had the most recommended Char Sio Bao. I liked the every bite of it. The pork fat was properly infused in it. I explored the taste of oyster at fatty bao by ordering The Fatty Oysters. They were served in four shells of oyster accompanied by pieces of lemon. Excellent I must admit. Oh how can I forget there desserts. Japanese cheese cake is a must. The slow cooked apple with ice cream was also very nice. I am really looking forward to my next visit in this place to explore the Sushis, PB&J and the Japanese cheesecake again. Highly recommended for every Bangalorian :)

          Landing at their air conditioned terrace area on a hot sunny afternoon was a relief. The decor is pretty colourful and lively and the happy staff compliments it. I must say, I was pretty surprised as the place was almost full on a weekday afternoon. The very interesting menu got us meat-lovers excited and we ordered a lot of things. Along with some beer, we ordered two portions of roast duck bao, California sushi roll with crab meat, beef and jalapeño gyoza and crispy fried squid. We dug into all the food armed with our chopsticks(that the super sweet waiter taught us how to use). The bao was delicious and melting in our mouths, I'm not a sushi fan but still didn't hate it. The gyoza(pan fried dumplings) were a winner for me, given i love beef. And the crunchy squid was great with cold beer. The meat was cooked really well and flavoured nicely in all the dishes. To top off the awesome and heavy lunch, we ordered a chocolate caramel fondant served with wassabi gelato(the gelato made me order it) which was a perfect end to the meal. This place is slightly expensive but is definitely worth it. I am coming here as many times as it takes for me to taste the whole menu XD and i am pretty sure the rating would be a perfect 5 then.
