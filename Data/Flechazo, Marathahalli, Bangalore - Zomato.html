          Few places make you feel at home right away, you start looking forward to not just the food but having a good time overall too. Flechazo is one such place.
        The decor, the lighting, the vibrance, the staff....these grab your attention much before the food does and hold it too.
        We landed up on a weekend afternoon, we were given the option for both the buffet and A la carte. My parents opted for the buffet spread, I had continental on my mind. The lunch buffet had Dahi ka Shorba and a decent salad platter to begin with. Starters are served at the table. There's also a Chaat/chaat shots conveyor that serves golgappa, dimsums and sushi among others. Red plate is Non-veg and the green is veg.
         I like that they have a good selection of vegetarian starters as well as the non-veg ones. The crispy Corn, Asian Cheese Potato, Paneer Multani Tikka find special mention. The paneer especially was soft, didn't have a rubbery bite to it. But what we found more amazing was the Cinnamon Honey Pineapple, that one of the servers was taking around Doner style, slicing off the fruit on to your plate.
        Live pasta counters are aplenty, but here you can make your own pizza too! And it doesnt end there, you will be spoilt for choice with the dessert offerings. Raspberry mousse, mini macaroons, brownie and, instant ice cream using liquid nitrogen. My recco is for the Paan ice cream, a must-try.
        For me, Flechazo scores brownie points on the factor that this place is comfortable to spend time with both your family and friends. It is recommended to reserve your table in advance as there can be a bit of waiting any day, but it's totally worth it!

          Love at first sight-Flechazo: "I do"
        Walk up the aisle and you will say "Yes" to this beautiful place.

        Location- right opposite to Rainbow hospital after emc2 junction.

        Ambiance- fine dining cum balcony seating with fairy lights instantly takes the charm to the next level.

        Concept- buffet with a complimentary beer/mocktail in Rs750 max per person.

        Service- it feels so good when each staff member really takes pride and owns the responsibility to ensure each guest is delighted and comes back.

        Food-We were told about the concept and offered our respective drink.
        Round 1: The first round starts with you walking to the "Food shots"-conveyor belt of Sushi, Chaat and dimsums. (Pls note green plate is veg and red one is nonveg).
        Round 2: Then comes your Veg &Nonveg starters served on your table. While all of them were delectable, my favourite was the Prawns and Honey cinnamon pineapple starter which was heavenly.
        Round 3: Walk upto the Pizza/Pasta counter and wear your Chef hats to prepare your own woodfire Pizzas. Take some few clicks too to show off your culinary skills.
        Round 4: If you are smart to pace your food intake till now, walk to the main buffet and serve yourself some delicious veg/nonveg food. Loved the biryani and Paneer.
        Round 5: Highly recommend the Nitrogen Icecream corner and specially the sumptuous Paan flavor. Its an experience of its own. Out of the world. Do try.

        If you are in a group, you may also go for a quick round of vodka/tequila shots as a staff member walks with a whistle to let you know about his arrival!!

        Loved the place and everything about it!! Highly recommended.
        PS:Do come early like 630ish before the space gets jam packed.

          FLECHAZO is bangalore's first MediterrAsia restaurant
        located in Outer Ring Road ,Marathahalli. The restaurant is just above Surya Nissan and opposite to Rainbow Children's Hospital.

        Flechazo has gained a lot of credibility for it's innovative concepts,ambience ,food and service.
        So one fine weekend when I got an invite from a friend to visit this place for Sunday lunch,I was super excited to finally explore this place 😃😃😃

        Like they say Every occasion is a time to celebrate 🎉🎊and where there is celebration there is food 🥗🍧🍰🌮🍕

        **Ambience**

        The ambiance is ultra modern with just the perfect lighting's and soft shades of blue and yellow interiors.
        The walls have photo frames with catchy slogans 😎

        There are two types of seating arrangements:

        Option 1: Cushion sofas -The blue shade ones are for group of 4 and the yellow ones can accommodate a large group of around 8-10 people.

        Option 2: Stylish chairs - Blue and yellow shades which again can accommodate a small to large group.

        The moment you enter this place you will be greeted by the colorful gola counter at the left 🍡😀, walk few steps and you will see Food Shots counter at the right​.

        In the front you have the Famous Smokin Cold counter🍧🍨 which at times looks hazy due to liquid nitrogen ☺️☺️

        Opposite to the Smokin Cold you have the hot and cold desserts section 🍰🍮🍉🍰 followed by Live counters serving Indian and Italian delicacies.

        They have Big O Section where one can try their skills in baking their own personal pizza 🍕🍕🍕

        The main course section is located in the inner corner of the place 😃

        **The Buffet Spread**

        The spread was lavish and consisted of huge varieties 😄

        MOCKTAILS:🍹🍹🍹

        •Green Apple Spritzer : My personal favorite of the 3 mocktails I tasted..It had nice flavours of green apple soda which complemented with meal.👍👌👌

        •Nitro Batata: This was something new for me. The mocktail is made of litchi and passion fruit served in a cocktail glass and garnished with rose..The mocktail is initially in liquid form which later gets solidified by adding liquid nitrogen .Nice concept👍👍👍

        •Fruit Punch: The classic fruity flavoured milk based mocktail.Tasted good 👍

        STARTERS:🍗🍗🍗

        ° Banjara Murg Tikka: tender pieces of chicken marinated in traditional slices..It tasted good 👍

        °Tossed Chicken in ginger basil sauce: the flavour of ginger with basil sause complemented the chicken 👍

        °Ghost Mughlai Seekh: Although I m not a mutton fan...The Seekh was tasty and would recommend to try this with mint chutney👍👍

        °Lasoni Tawa Machi: Mildly flavoured fish starter..It was good .Must try👍👍👍

        °Singapore Prawns: The Prawns were juicy and little spicy,taste was good .Must try👍👍👍

        °Chicken Shewarma: They have DIY concept ,where first they shreded the chicken pieces live on the table and then offer Pita bread with various dips..Shewarma was good and must try👍👍👍

        °Prawns Tandoori: Spicy prawns ,different flavours.

        ° Fish chilli: nice flavours👍👍

        ° Chicken in sesame sauce: Crispy chicken in sesame based sauce👍👍

        °Cinnamon Honey Pineapple: The staff would cut this live on the table.The pineapple with honey was juicy and had the right amount of cinnamon..It was simple awesome 👌👌👌

        ° Crispy Corn: I did try few veg starters and crispy corn was good👍

        ° Veg dumplings: Nice and tasty 😃

        MAIN COURSE:🥘🥘🥘
        They have a lavish spread with 2 soups,6 salads,5 non veg main course and 9 veg main course.
        Since I had an enormous amount of starters and had to make room for their delectable desserts.I had only few items from main course mainly rice items.

        •Murg Dum Biryani: The Dum flavours were enhanced and the chicken pieces were too good 👍👍

        •Veg Subz dry fruit biryani: The traditional vegetable biryani is a must try👍👍

        •Steam rice and Masoor dal: Ended the maincourse with Rice and dal 😃

        FOOD COUNTERS:🍕🍤🍥

        •Food Shots:

        The counter has an assortment of chat,sushis, Dimsums and Tapas placed over a conveyor belt which keeps on rotating.

        I tried Paani poori,honey carrot sushi,cucumber and cheese bellpepper,tempura sushi roll and Prawns in Thai peanut sause.
        I liked the prawns and Paani poori the most 👌👌👌

        • The Big O and live counters:

        They offer freshly baked pizzas with wide variety of toppings plus they also have bake your pizza concept.
        In the live counters they had mutton bhunna,Tawa paneer and khulcha.
        They also offer fresh pasta in penne/fussilli with red or white sauce and as per the toppings requested.

        As was already full,I skipped these counters😐😐😐

        • Indian Desserts:

        Coin Jalebis: Hot and crispy Jalebis were deep fried and then given to eat.The Jalebis had the right amount of sweetness and tasted nice👍👍

        Moong dal halwa ,gulab jamoon and phirni were also good 👍👍

        •International Desserts:

        Macroons: My favourite,they had assorted macroons and they were delectable😃👌👌

        Walnut brownie could have been better,it had become too hard 😐😐

        Pineapple Pastry was bang on👌👌👌

        Rasberry Souffle was good👍

        Fruit custard was also nice👍👍

        °Smokin cold desserts:

        It was a visual treat to watch the chef make those beautiful ice creams using liquid nitrogen.
        The highlight was pan ice cream,what was interesting was to actually see the chef take a pan leaf in a separate bowl add liquid nitrogen to it and then crush the pan leaves as garnish on the pan ice cream.
        Pan ice cream was spot on and highly recommended 👌👌👌👌
        I so tried chocolate brownie which was chocolaty and had brownie chunks in it 😃😃
        Last but not the least Mix Berry punch was similar to blackberry ice cream.in taste,so thumps up ,I mean thumbsup😁😁👍👍

        **Service**

        The service is impeccable by the highly trained staff who are courteous and have good knowledge about the food being served.Despite the huge crowd,they were giving immediate attention and were on there toes to do their best.
        The chef is very polite and is happy to accept feedbacks.
        Special thanks to the manager Suresh Rao for explaining the concept of Flechazo 😃

        **Value for Money**
        The price for 2 comes down to 1200 ,which is a steal deal ,considering the huge buffet spread with the addition of liquid nitrogenated ice creams..It's totally worth every penny spent😃😃😃

        Overall Rating:
        Location:4/5
        Ambience:4.5/5
        Food:4.5/5
        VFM: 5/5

        Will I visit again:
        Yes,I would highly recommend this place for family dinners,team parties,special occasions or just come long empty stomach to get pampered with Flechazo experience😃😃😃

          “The meal is not over when I’m full. The meal is over when I hate myself” – Louis C.K.

        And, that's exactly how I felt, when I walked out of Flechazo. With my legs faltering and my appetite stable, I dragged my two left feet out of the door.

        I'm not a huge buffet fan, but my little sister is. Having heard a lot about Flechazo, I decided to give it a try with the family anyway. I mean, what's there to lose?

        About Flechazo :

        Flechazo, in simple terms means "Love at first sight or your love interest" in Spanish. They have a mix of both Mediterranean and Asian Cuisine to offer. Hence, the name " Mediterr-Asian" cuisine. It's a bungee jumping experience, with so much that catches the eye, you wanna take a dip in everything.

        They also break a leg when it's your special day and start celebrating along with you. "Oo, tera happy birthday, Oo Tera happy birthday. Aaj hogi party"

        Decor : The decor is industrial chic, with heavy emphasis on comfort as well. The upholstery is a beautiful palette of neutrals and aqua. The ambience is inevitably cheery. I could do a 100 jumping jacks in here and not get bugged. The lighting also uplifts the place by being not too harsh and not too dim as well. The sofas are layered with plush cushions and that is just great. 👌🏻

        Hospitality : One of the best, I daresay. What joy it is, to dine in here. The staff are courteous and on their toes. Have a great, in-depth knowledge of the menu. Special mention to Rajat and Ankush, for hosting us with so much fervour.

        The Grub Story : Being a buffet restaurant, there isn't much that I can go on in details about a dish, because there were so many.

        The innovative way the restaurant is designed will also bowl you over. They have a neat pizza section, furnace et al. You get to create your own customised pizza, with the vigilant chef by your side of course! Roll it, top it and push it into the furnace.

        A little shaved ice or gola 🍧 counter, where you get to indulge in the childhood favourite.

        They also have a rotating counter for dimsums, sushi, prawn shooters and gol guppas. Conveyor belt moves around and keeps getting filled by the chef 👨‍🍳 Reminds you of the long queues at the airport, but this was one queue, I enjoyed waiting in.

        Here were a few favourites from each section.

        Spicy Cajun Pineapple : I love Pineapples!! I'm Patrick.. I live under one 😋😋 This baby was so well grilled and spiced, I just couldn't stop. The characteristic sweet-sour tone prevailed. The Cajun spices were coated well, could you easily taste the black pepper and paprika.

        Crispy Corn and Spring Roll : One of my favourite. The crispy corn, was such a fantastic thing to mindlessly munch on. Little pearls of spicy corn nibbles just had me going. The cabbage spring rolls were dainty and fair enough.

        Mezzeh Platter with Pita : I love the Mezzeh always. The beauty about it, is that, it always impresses me. This one was no different, with the silky Hummus that stood colossally delish.

        Singaporean fried Mushroom : Where were you? Yes! Red, Succulent, Deep, Spicy, Flavourful and Mushroom. 'Nuff said, isn't it.? The Singaporean Mushroom just was one of the best starters I've had in a long time. The button mushrooms were just bursting with flavour from the sauce that coated it.

        Paneer Pasanda Tikka : Creamy and grilled just the way, it's supposed to be. This one, surprised me. Usually, I'm never the one, who would expect a buffet restaurant to dish out soft and juicy cubes of cottage cheese, for the mere thought of volume. But, I dunno how, these guys just presented to us, succulent well marinated and grilled cubes of Paneer. Happy? Oh yes!

        Multani Gosht : This starter was apparently the most appreciated from the lot by the sister and the hubby. The Mutton was soft and coated well with the thick Multani sauce which usually has a mix of garam masala and ripened tomatoes.

        Grilled Cilantro Fish : Quite a mouthful, is as they said. The simplicity of the ingredients made the fish to hero itself.

        Chicken Shawarma : The hubster, just loved it, had no complaints. Said, the juicy character of the chicken stayed intact and the spice levels were spot on.

        Pizza : Mainstream Pizza, is such a passé. Handmade, thin crust and cheesy pizzas are the "IN" thing. Happy was I, to make my own beautiful margherita pizza with generous sprinklings of cheese and Basil. Loved the pizza and the experience.

        Veg Sushi : Back in the days of my predominant Carnivore side, I never had the heart or the gut to devour Non Veg Sushi.. it's something, i never could get around to. Tuna, Salmon or any meat in my sushi. So I've always been a Vegetarian FutoMaki fan. I enjoyed the Maki Roll over here. The beautiful pungency of the wasabi with the cucumber filling, wrapped in a nori. Made my day!!
