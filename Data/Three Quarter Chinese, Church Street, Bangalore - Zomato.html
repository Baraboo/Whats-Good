        Rated
          We'd eaten here a long time ago, and the buffet was good then. After much contemplation we decided to go here again for lunch on a weekday

        Location:
        On the so called food street, easy to find.
        Below amoeba

        Ambiance:
        Looked like a big dining hall. Big space, but only 2-3 tables were occupied.
        Almost all the plates had some kind of stains

        Starters:
        So, in most places, the starters are served on the table, and this was the case here as well, the last time we came.
        We'd to go take them ourselves from the buffer counter, which meant they were not fresh and cold.
        No starter was any good to brag about, but to name a few:

        Chicken wings:
        Cold and bland. That's the best description I can give

        Fish Chinese style:
        There was a small piece of fish coated heavily with cornflour

        Tawa fish:
        It was stale and had a stench to it

        Malai Kabab:
        This was the best of the non veg lot

        Onion and cheese wonton:
        Oily, but decent

        Kachori:
        This was surprisingly good along with the Dahi vada

        momos:
        Were raw on the underside

        Shawarma:
        No where close to a shawarma. Mayo and salad with bit of raw chicken

        We asked them to get a couple of hot starters, but they were just reheated and hot which made things worse

        Mains:
        Chicken and egg fried rice:
        Rice was undercooked and were hard. Like they were reheated

        Prawns Chinese gravy:
        Prawns were not fresh and were smelling

        Mutton biriyani:
        Rice was okay, not like a good biriyani

        Palak paneer:
        This was pretty decent

        Didn't try too much after this

        Dessert:
        Blueberry tart:
        This was good. Crisp and a nice hit of blueberry

        Caramel custard:
        Very eggy, not fluffy and light like how a caramel custard should be

        Moong Dal halwa:
        This was again good. One of the few good things in an otherwise disappointing meal

        Angoori rabdi:
        The milk seemed split

        Apart from this there was ice cream, some cheesecakes, chocolate fondue, honey dussan noodles and fried banana, which were all average or below average.

        All in all, at ₹721 per head this place can be avoided as there are much better options in a similar price range.

        Ambiance: 3/5
        Service: 4/5
        Taste: 4/5
        Value: 4.5/5

        Choosing somewhere to eat in Church Street is always a momentous decision so it was with an open mind we checked out Three Quarters Chinese on the ground floor of the Amoeba Complex.


        The first thing that strikes you is the sheer size of the restaurant. Huge seating area with equally large serving sections for Indian, Chinese, soup, Salads, live noodle station, dumpling counter, tandoor plates, chaat station, dessert selection... the list goes on with a massive choice of cuisines and tastes.


        I found the range far more "Three Quarters Indian and One Quarter Chinese" more appropriate actually, given the melange of Indian meets Chinese.


        The service is particularly fast given the size of the place but I would have appreciated being made aware that the soft drinks are chargeable. This should be pointed out to patrons.


        Sunday brunch came to ~1700 for two which is very fair given how much we ate. Skipped breakfast and was still feeling completely stuffed at 11pm the same day!


        One of the best things about TQC is there really is something for everyone and every taste.
