          Brigade road is like a go to place all the time and saw this new place called commmmmmmmunity 😂 very catchy!
        Being regulars at DTP, we thought of checking this place out since we first saw it but didn't really get a chance.
        This Sunday afternoon though we did get a chance and drove right up to it!
        As soon as you enter the gate, you find it's evidently cooler than one step outside the gate, probably has something to do with the awesome piano fountain or just the greenery right there.
        Inside was a little stuffy and low lit and definitely needs better air conditioning.
        Moving on, we went in and saw that the place was huuuge which is awesome because it could accommodate all the crowd that was there.
        We were shown to our table and presented with the f&b menu.
        Being beer lovers, without much thought we ordered 2 tuborg pints and after some thought the starters.
        For starters, we ordered pork belly and crispy fish.
        Halfway through the beer, pork belly was served. That is, service is quick!!
        One bite of that heavenly dish and we were shocked for it was too good to be real! Perfectly cooked and perfect sauces. The belly was like an explosion of awesomeness in our mouths. And we gobbled up without even talking. Lol.
        Then in sometime came the crispy fish. It was good. The quantity of tartar sauce could've been better but as a whole, it was worth the price.
        With the second round of beers now was the time for main course, I ordered a volcano pasta, and my friend ordered a southern fried beef steak.
        The beef steak was like Kentucky fried beef which was good and quite nicely cooked and served with mash and garlic bread and some stirfry zucchini.
        Volcano pasta was really volcanic as it was amazingly cheesy spaghetti with a BBQ sauce chicken steak. The steak was more of a grilled patty of chicken flavoured with garlic.
        Main courses were not like we expected.
        Now coming to the rating,
        Ambience-3.5
        Staff and service-4.5
        Food-4
        Overall experience- 4
        Good for people from all age groups as there's no blasting music and stuff.
        Will recommend to try once and definitely try the pork belly!!❤

          This place is the only place I've seen soo crowded on a Sunday night in recent times. Value for money written all over it. A friend suggested this place and it is just brilliant.         I had the chicken nachos, prawns butter garlic, classic spaghetti bolognase, crispy fish, orange chicken (not quite sure of the name) and a whole lot of beer, since it was summer. Absolutely loved the classic spaghetti bolognase.

        The service is very quick. Only minus point is there is no AC and the low seating where we sat, we needed to remove our footwear and if you are wearing short dresses, it's kinda of a task to get yourself in a comfortable position.

        Overall, a must must try.

        Rated
          Extra 0.5 for the pricing. The pricing of the offerings of the place is lower than the other places, yet the stuff offered is much better than some other places.

        Location:
        Excellent. Diagonally opposite to Nagarjuna restaurant on residency road, and beside Gateway hotel.

        Ambiance:
        Can do better. They have long table and bench on either side, like a college canteen. They also have a few separate tables around.
        One negative is no AC. During the summer afternoon it can get hot, like it was when we went there on a Sunday afternoon.

        Drinks:
        Very reasonably priced.

        Shooters:
        Available from ₹150/- only!!

        Brain buster:
        It felt very mild. Could taste more of the diluters.

        Mango grove:
        In this you can clearly taste the alcohol: old monk.
        Simple shooter with mango juice and old monk. You get the rum first and then the relief of the mango juice.

        Mocktail:

        Passion delight:
        As the name says it, it's a passion fruit based mocktail. Tasted pretty good.

        Mango maniac:
        Mango biased fruit punch id call it.
        But tasted very good, even the one year old with us loved it.

        Starters:
        Chilli Texan fries:
        Very average. Under-seasoned, and the salsa added on top makes it very soggy.

        Cheesy salsa nachos:
        Ordinary again, can be made much better.

        Fiery basil mushroom:
        The basil in the name doesn't disappoint.
        Not very spicy though as it should be, going by the name. This had a whole lotta other toppings as well.

        Deviled egg with spicy peanut:
        Good try to make something interesting, but just off the mark. It was a little soggy and again, not spicy.

        Crispy chicken tenders:
        Oily, but crunchy.
        5 pieces of fried, crunchy chicken with a nice herby dip. Pretty good.

        Pepper glazed chicken:
        Disappointing. Over cooked chicken and lots and lots of batter. Slight hit of pepper.

        Butter and cheesy fish:
        Very good. Creamy, cheesy and tasty.
        Basa fish is used to make this dish.
        The slight let down was that the first time we ordered it we loved it so much that we ordered it again, however the second time it was below par compared to first one. The cut of the fish was different and it was less creamy and saucy. It was still good, but because the first dish set the bar so high we were slightly disappointed second time around. And you won't unbelievably it's priced at ₹160/-
        That's easily half the cost as it would be in most other places.

        Main:
        Just tried the Pasta arrabbiata
        Sauce was good, but the pasta itself was a bit overdone.

        Overall decent place, reasonably priced.
        Apart from one standout dish, a couple of mocktails and one good shooter, other dishes were pretty average.
