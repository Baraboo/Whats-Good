        Rated
          Let's just start by saying..this place has the best offers for foodies. The unlimited scheme seems like something out of a dream, 450 bucks for an unlimited variety of pizzas and desserts.Their options had me spoilt for choice!Hence this place is mostly filled with college and office crowds.Here is the following list of freshly baked goodies we devoured, stay tuned, this is going to be a long one!
         
        Vegetarian Pizzas
        1.Basil Pesto, Tomato and Feta
        2.Red Grapes with Balsamic Reduction(This one was a dessert trial)

        Non-Vegetarian Pizzas
        1.Wasabi Mayo Chicken(ordered 2 of these)
        2.Cream Roasted Chicken
        3.Crispy Chicken Calzone
        4.Prawn Pesto
        5.Roast Chicken
        6.Chicken, Pineapple, and Jalapeno

        Drinks
        1.Green Apple Cooler
        2.Orange Mint Cooler

        As both my parents and I have a ingrained connection to spicy food, our very first concern was if the pizzas would be spicy enough.And the funny thing is, even though we ordered the flamiest from the bunch, we ended up finishing the Tobasco.One other aspect of Onesta pulled us in, all of their varieties came in thin crusts so we could eat A LOT as the crust tends to take up a lot of space.Starting with Prawn Pesto, Roast Chicken and Chicken, Pineapple and Jalapeno we decided to play it safe. The latter two were most impressive with the Roast being beautifully crusty and with an even spread of chicken, though I would've liked a bit more cheese. The C,P and J on the other, was the only one we took without any accompaniments, it was the perfect amount of spicy and sweet.Think, sweet and sour chicken negating the nauseating pungency of honey and the addition of a crust and cheese, so this one was a delight The Prawn Pesto needed a more butter battered prawn reduction and way more pesto, but otherwise not too bad.Now, coming to my father's favorite and mine as well, the Wasabi Mayo. Though dad was doubtful about this one, I wanted to try something new and it in fact paid off, with us ordering another one with the next set. The wasabi was absolute perfection with the creamy consistency of the mayonnaise combining the hit of spice with the coolant of the mayo. Frankly, the chicken was just an addition. This is a "come back" dish for sure.

        Perhaps the only "pizza" that disappointed me was the Crispy chicken calzone.They just misused the word because I believe the actual composition of the dish consists of meat with veggies encases in a flour pocket that has been sealed with folds, more of a filo pastry texture and then baked till perfection, but this was deep fried and kind of a pain to chew.But let's just take this one as "experimental".

        The second set was the calzone mentioned above, Cream Roasted Chicken and the Basil Pesto, Tomato and Feta and it was again a success apart from the former. The CRC, the cheesiest of all of these pizzas came out in a lovely string, just like the ones in the movies. Cheese sauce is the BEST.The ONLY vegetarian alternative (because barbarians need salad too) the Basil Pesto, Tomato and Feta was average. I personally, would have liked more Feta, because of my love for an array of cheeses and my need to try most of them.Finally, the dessert pizza Red Grapes with balsamic reduction was the most unique. With the grape baked to retain it's round shape, yet not spilling its juices over the pizza, the tanginess just right with a slight burnt trail and the cheese cutting off the sweetness at the right point.A good combination of ingredients, more so, impressive execution. 

        Coming to the drinks, both the Orange mint and the Green Apple were refreshing and just what we needed to wash our massive meal down. The Green Apple though, was a triumphant winner.

        Not too much about the desserts, not really a massive fan, but besides the Cappucino Mousse(which was like sugar in a waffle cup), the rest were very well done.My favorite was the Tiramisu in a cup with a shortbread biscuit, not overtly sweet, not bitter.Just right.

        Food-8/10
        Service-6/10
        Value for money-8.5/10

        Overall, a beautiful, bustling place to visit on my birthday.A truly great discovery.

          I was invited by them for a complimentary meal so visited this place on a weeknight and it rained heavily that night.Located on CMH road,Indiranagar.It is diagonally opposite to the ICICI bank indiranagar branch.This is a roof top restaurant and they have lift facility.We were welcomed by Mr Joseph.The restaurant is done up in a very lovely way,with lots of plants,good lighting and also an open counter where you can how your food is being cooked.There was also a huge display of desserts where you can choose your favourite ones.

        We had a menu that was specially curated by the chef.we had brushchetta,chicken tenders in barbeque sauce, crispy chicken with sweet chilli sauce, fish finger with garlic mayo,tropical fish with salsa,sausage roll, fried prawns sauteed in sauce and finally bacon wrapped prawns...these were just the starters and we ordered orange mint cooler,virgin mojito and lemon iced tea.

        My favourites were the brushchetta with sun dried tomatoes n olive oil,crispy chicken with sweet chilli sauce,bacon wrapped prawns,fish finger with mayo.the bacon wrapped prawns were just delicious the fish finger was made of fresh basa fish.The drinks were great and refereshing.

        Next came the main course we had the their signature dishes.Chicken lasagne and prawn ricotta ravioli, in both sage n butter sauce And arrabiatta.I'm already on love with the lasagne here and I was not disappointed here for sure.Its served with a tangy salad which is an absolute favourite.I take my pasta seriously and favoured the ravioli in sage butter sauce better.It's a must try along with the lasagne.

        Coming to the desserts,since we were already full we choose only 4desserts.The ricotta cheesecake,double chocolate cake,the kiwi cheese mousse and the apple cinnamon mousse.they were so light and yum.Wish I had space for more.Do try their desserts,you will be spoilt for choice.

        They also make and sell breads I think as they had various shaped breads displayed on the counter.Also they would give you a complimentary bread basket with butter and mayo.

        Although onesta is famous for its pizzas,do try the other stuff they have to offer other wise you will be missing out on a lot.The pricing is just right for the portions they offer,the service,staff and food is just top notch.
