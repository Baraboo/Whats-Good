          " DELHI FOOD FESTIVAL @ SULTANS OF SPICE! "

        Delhi once being the capital of the Mughal Empire marks the origin of the Mughlai cuisine which is till date a distinctive reminiscence of the Mughal influence in our country. Sultans of Spice endeavors to incorporate the royal cuisines belonging to the old Delhi Sultanate that also includes Afghani cuisine derived from Kabul and Punjabi cuisine due to the dominance of the Punjabi community in Delhi. An integral part of the BluPetal Hotel and located in the busy streets of Koramangala, Sultans of Spice is a quintessential North Indian restaurant that focuses greatly on food and hospitality. Being an ardent lover of the cuisine myself, my friend and I had recently been here to try out their ongoing Delhi Food Festival lunch buffet.

        As we walked in, we couldn’t help but notice the vivid ambiance depicting the renowned street food culture of Delhi. At the entrance, a spacious stall was positioned which dished out an array of stuffed paranthas, tawa specialties and thandais. A second live counter present inside customized the real flavor of Delhi street food i.e. the chaats. The entire place was edified by adding elements illustrating the historic Delhi culture. I really liked how the area was divided into different sections and named after famous old Delhi neighborhoods such as Chandni Chowk, Nehru Place, Janpath, Connaught Place, Khan Market, Palika Bazaar, Lajpat Nagar, Sarojini Nagar and so on. The rest of the décor was a combination of blue, white and brown furnishing, mock paper lanterns adorning significant monumental prints and royal backdrops.

        We were shown to our table which happened to be in Chandni Chowk adjacent to Connaught Place. 😬 As the humor settled in, we commenced an elaborate luncheon with our welcome drinks – a refreshing tangy Aam Panna and sweet Malai Lassi with rose syrup. We also ordered a couple of summer mocktails from their a la carte menu and I am certainly going back for these. I would strongly recommend the Ruby Chill and Bay Rider which are not only the most enticing beverages but will also have one reaching for his/her phone to capture its picturesque outlook. We also tried the Mango Meridian and Melon Lemonade that were equally invigorating. All the beverages contained seasonal flavors and despite everything it was light and exhilarating. 

        The buffet counter included an extensive selection of 10 vegetarian and non vegetarian appetizers. First up was the Nalli Kalimirch ka Saar which was a peppery mutton soup with a broth like consistency followed by a series of starters. Crunchy Chicken was well done with a crispy exterior and juicy inside. Chatpata Chicken Kebab and Ajwaini Macchi Tikka were the usual standard affair. Gosht Dwara Kebab bearing a striking resemblance to Seekh Kebab was a tad dry even though it was pretty good. Sarsonwali Jhinga was tasteful and I remember gorging on quite a few of them. The vegetarian starters were more or less at par with the non-vegetarian options. Aloo Mukki Chutneywala had a chatpata outer coating which hit all the right spots with every bite. Anjeer aur Matar ki Tikki might be an eccentric concoction but surely something new to look out for. Mazedaar Bhutte is their very own interpretation of the popular Crispy Corn but the excessive batter compromised the freshness of the dish. Tandoori Chilli Paneer and Malai Kalimirch Mushroom were quite disappointing due to its rubbery texture and bland flavor. 

        Before moving on to main course, we had to hit the live street food section that covered some of the legendary restaurants of Delhi - stuffed Paranthas from Paranthe Wali Gali, Karim’s Paneer/Chicken Changezi, Dilli Kulche aur Chole/Gosht Nihari and Delhi chatpata Chaats. Out of 12 choices of paranthas, I tried the Egg, Paneer and Khajoor variants. The Khajoor was a noteworthy option and a must try! I couldn’t really differentiate between egg and paneer as the thick parantha dough was all I could taste. However, the Gosht Nihari was a great accompaniment to the soft Kulche. The mutton pieces in the spicy gravy were extremely tender and I polished it away with the Kulche. The Chicken Changezi was another favorite of mine. The boneless chunks of chicken were perfectly cooked in a piquant spice mix and I just couldn’t stop nibbling at it. Finally, the chaats we got to sample were Raj Kachori, Dahi Bhalla and Karela Chaat. I have never really liked Delhi chaats before as being a Bengali I have always loved and believed chaats to be a specialty of my native (Calcutta). Therefore I might be biased on this front. So go ahead and indulge in all the 14 varieties they have to offer! 🙂

        At last, main course - this is that portion of the meal which never fails to dishearten me but to my utter surprise, it was brilliant. 😛 Beginning with the vegetarian choices, Paneer Butter Masala was a balanced composition of sweet and spice. The creamy Dal Makhani goes really well with both rice and breads. Sarson Saag Malai Kofta was a rich Mughlai preparation and a commendable dish. Ananas aur Pudine ka Pulao was again another outlandish item on the menu and entails an acquired taste. Among the non-vegetarian options, I enjoyed the Saagwala Meat. The succulent meat easily separated from the bone and for all haters out there, this is probably the best approach to consume spinach. 😉 Harimirch aur Nariyal ka Jhinga was an Indianised version of Thai Curry, coconut milk being the hero ingredient besides the tiny prawns. As usual I leave the best for the last and yes, I am talking about the Muradabadi Shahi Gosht Biriyani. Off late, I have developed a taste for the Lucknowi Biriyani as it is not crazy spicy yet retains the necessary flavors and essence of biriyani. The Biriyani here was an impeccable version of the same and hence, a must-have in my opinion!

        I have always given way too much attention to desserts (as it is sort of my birthright to do so 😁) and therefore, this time without further ado I shall straightaway get into it. Despite being full to the brim, I did manage a bite of almost all the desserts laid out at the counter. For me, Gulab Jamoon Pistewali and Gud ka Phirni were absolutely delectable. Mocha Mousse with a subtle hint of coffee is worth a mention as well. Other available options were Rasmalai, Gajar ka Halwa, Malai Kachori, Mango Cheesecake, Chocolate Mudcake, Ice Creams and cut fruits. Do not miss the live Jalebi counter tucked away at a corner serving flavored jalebis – apple, pineapple and mirchi. I tried the regular one which rendered quite the crunch and was generously doused in the sugar syrup. Also, they do a divine Rabdi Falooda which is a masterpiece of Giani Di Hatti situated in Chandni Chowk, Old Delhi. Brace yourself for a Sweet Explosion! 🤗

        I have been ranting on for quite some time but judging by the number of dishes I got to try; it wasn’t much of a choice. :D Well wrapping it up, the service is prompt and pricing is reasonable. The Delhi Food Festival buffet is priced at Rs. 639+ taxes for lunch and Rs. 693+ taxes for dinner. It is on till 2nd April, 2017 and I would suggest all the foodies to rush there while they still can as this is a one-time opportunity to experience such a lavish spread of authentic Mughlai cuisine like never before! 😀
