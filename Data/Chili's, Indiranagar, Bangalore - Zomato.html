          If Scrouge McDuck ever started a restaurant, this would be it. Why you ask? Let me elaborate (with photos attached)
        The first photos are of fish tacos. And by fish, I mean one sad barely spiced shrimp which I only realised was in there when my friend appraised me of it. It is to be noted that Tex-mex taco shell traditionally fried or toasted while the Mexican ones aren't. But that can be ignored. To accompany the shrimp, and because this is allegedly Mexican cuisine, they also "generously" put one strip of avocado. Yes one strip (you can see that in the photo). A generous (by comparison) serving of black beans and mint rice completed the dish. How did it taste you ask? Of nothing really. My taste buds could not locate the shrimp or the avocado and most of what I tasted was the taco shell.

        Next comes the nachos. That's the second pic in case you failed to recognise it because I sure didn't recognise what that was and ask my friend if she really did order nachos. So yes that other pic is that of their nachos. Has no one in their staff ever seen a serving of nachos? Please, I implore you Chili's Indiranagar, Google up "nachos" because what you served wasn't anything like what a plate of nachos is supposed to look like.
        For 400 bucks, we got 8 pieces of tortilla (yes Uncle Scrouge would be so proud) topped with oil and cheese (good cheese though if I may add), cream and a niggardly serving of salsa. Seriously, who skimps on salsa? It is extremely cheap to make. How much of their margins would they have to sacrifice to triple the quantity of salsa they served?

        The drinks were good and no complaints on that end. The staff was courteous and the ambience was quite good, unlike Chili's in America where it's a bugdet restaurant chain.

        I had high hopes but I was disappointed. After those two items, I decided to save myself any further disappointment and decided to head to Mother Clickers. I don't know why they were skimping on everything; heck I asked for fork and spoon and was provided one of each although there were 2 of us at the table. It would have been hilarious if I didn't have to pay for this.

          Dropped by at Chili's on a late Friday night. Been to the Mumbai (Powai) outlet a little too many times so I was looking forward to similar standards.
        I called for a mocktail, specifying at the time of the order that I want whatever is the LEAST sweet. I was given some orange mocktail that had nothing but concentrate all the way up to more than half the glass.
        We called for mushroom soup at the beginning, not knowing at that time that it was going to be the best part of our meal that night.
        We called for Cajun Alfredo pasta which was super bland. We sent it back and asked them to season it but it pretty much came back the same as before.
        The veg burger I ordered had absolutely no sauce or veggies. Just a patty, two onion rings and the tiniest shred of lettuce in there. It was dryer than ever. When I asked if I could have some mayo or anything at all basically the waiter said this is how the burger is supposed to be.
        The tex mex bowl that we ordered was the nicest of them all. Although even for this we specifically asked them to not put carrots as one of our fellow diners was allergic. The way this meal was going, I wasn't even surprised when we had enormous chunks of carrot right on top.
        The quesadilla was absolutely saltless.

        The manager however apologised for everything and offered us a virgin mojito on the house. It was nice of him but then again it didn't do anything to salvage out hellish meal.

          What a wonderful way to celebrate a friend's birthday by visiting a place which serves good food. Isn't it?
        We decided to give Chili's Indiranagar a try after giving it soo much thought. We had to pick a place which was open all throughout the day cause we ended up having lunch at around 4:30. Chili's is an expensive restaurant but at the same time they serve in very large quantities and worth every penny! Ambience is really good. Cool elegant seatings. The music kept us going which is like a must for a place like this.
        We went during happy hours soo we had buy 1 Get 1 on drinks soo each of us had like 2-3 blender's special before we actually ordered any food.
        Food:

        1. New Smoked Chicken Quesadilla: It was served alongside some ice cold lettuce and really yummy garlic dip. The Quesadilla was hot with spicy bell peppers and the smoked chicken was beautiful. Really loved the dish.

        2. The New Baja Fish Tacos: Though the fish was perfect and full of flavour, the dish as a whole wasn't really amazing! Too much salsa on top and Served alongside black beans and some cilantro rice. Both of which were disappointing. Hence we cudnt rate the place a 5. 🙄

        3. Baby Back ribs: Was one of the best ribs I've had till date. The meat just fell right off the bone! Served alongside some crispy yummy fries and corn on cob. The ribs was cooked and drenched in a yummy BBQ sauce and we just cudnt smile on how good the dish was. The dish was perfect 😍🤗

        4. Philadelphia Melting Cheese Stake: As the name suggests, oozing cheese in a medium done steak. 😍👌. Had a really lovely mash on the side which complemented the dish beautifully. The mushrooms and bell peppers on top gave it more punch to it. The steak was juicy when it was hot but at the end it was too tough. Nonetheless Amazingly tasty and worth the try.

        Service was wonderful and the overall bill for all these was 3.5k which is not pocket friendly but can surely give it a chance. Valet parking soo not to worry abt car spaces. Really good music. Would surely visit the place with a bigger crowd. 😊
